Connector guide

Introduce Solvyr when someone owns a document backlog that needs to become AI-ready.

Public and semi-public ecosystem builders, ROMs, municipalities, archives, libraries, research networks, and regional innovation teams can help by connecting Solvyr to real corpus owners with bounded work.

Good introductions

  • Teams preparing PDFs or text for RAG, search, evals, or agents.
  • Organizations with a recurring corpus refresh or re-indexing need.
  • Archives, libraries, or agencies with clean digital document sets.
  • AI product teams waiting on usable corpus output.

Weak introductions

  • Generic curiosity about AI infrastructure without a workload.
  • Highly sensitive data cases without a safe sample.
  • Realtime serving or chatbot hosting needs.
  • Broad platform migration requests.

Suggested intro

Short, concrete, workload-first.

"Solvyr is running controlled Fast Corpus pilots: clean PDFs or text backlogs to AI-ready JSONL with chunks, embeddings, stable IDs, and a run report. This may fit if you have a document backlog or recurring corpus refresh."

Why connectors matter

The larger story is infrastructure, but the first proof is a workload.

Solvyr may be relevant to European execution capacity and sovereignty conversations, but the practical first step is still a representative document sample and a bounded Fast Corpus run.
