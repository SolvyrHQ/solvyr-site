About Solvyr

Fast Corpus sells completed corpus work. Controlled distributed execution is the company underneath.

Solvyr is in a founder-led, controlled trial phase. The current public offer is narrow on purpose: clean PDF and text backlogs to AI-ready JSONL, chunks, embeddings, stable IDs, a run report, and a clear starting price for standard batches. For archives and public teams, it also creates a practical way to evaluate European execution control around a real workload.

What is real now

Controlled async document/text corpus processing, pilot scoping, visible run evidence, predictable starting-unit pricing, and honest fit/non-fit boundaries.

What is being built

A distributed execution system with central scheduling, bounded tasks, retries, durable status, explainable results, and European execution control for workloads that fit.

What is not claimed

No broad self-serve platform, generic GPU rental, enterprise SLA, OCR-heavy support, or sensitive-data processing by default.

Why this shape

A concrete workload is the honest way to prove the system.

Solvyr is not trying to sell infrastructure in the abstract. Fast Corpus gives buyers, connectors, and agents one concrete backlog outcome, output bundle, and cost basis to evaluate before any broader platform or sovereignty conversation.

Current phase

Controlled trials, representative samples, and capped runs.

A good first conversation starts with document count, page count, input type, desired output, data sensitivity, recurrence, and whether a safe representative sample can be shared.
